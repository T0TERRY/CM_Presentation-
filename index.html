Overview

What is This Strategy?

Digital Platforms

Online job portals are web-based platforms that serve as bridges between employers and job seekers, making recruitment entirely digital and borderless.

How They Work

Companies post vacancies and candidates submit applications online — eliminating the need for physical visits, paper CVs, and geographic restrictions.

04 / 06
Challenges

Disadvantages

High Competition Hundreds of candidates apply for the same role, making it harder to get noticed.
Fake & Scam Listings Not all postings are genuine — fraudulent ads risk personal data exposure.
No Face-to-Face Interaction Lack of personal contact can make it difficult to assess culture fit.
Weak Résumés Get Ignored Poorly crafted CVs are filtered out by ATS systems before any human reviews them.
Employer Non-Response Many applicants hear nothing back, causing discouragement and uncertainty.

Popular Platforms

Banks, NGOs, and private companies use these portals to efficiently recruit qualified talent across the country.

Practical Example

A graduate from Royal University of Phnom Penh can:

1

Create a profile & upload their CV on BongThom or LinkedIn

2

Search & filter jobs by industry, salary & location

3

Apply to multiple companies online — no physical visit required

4

Receive interview invitations directly via email or message
